1. Executive Summary
What Nexa Labs is building and why this document exists.
Nexa Labs is being built as a Jamaica-born technology company focused on practical AI products that help 
people browse, create, understand information, and complete digital tasks faster. The flagship product family is 
Nexa AI and Nexa Browser. Nexa AI is a general AI chatbot experience available through the web, and Nexa 
Browser is a deeper browser-integrated experience where Nexa AI can become a contextual assistant inside the 
browsing workflow.
The goal is not to copy existing AI platforms. The goal is to create a product that combines chat, browser context, 
task assistance, web productivity, safety awareness, and user preferences into one integrated experience. A user 
should be able to ask Nexa AI to write a resume, summarize a page, complete a form, explain a website, check 
site safety signals, draft messages, and help with everyday digital work.
Core idea
Nexa AI can exist as a chatbot across regular browsers, while Nexa Browser gives Nexa AI a deeper assistant 
role with page context, browser commands, and web task execution.
 Nexa AI: the AI assistant/chatbot available through the web and future apps.
 Nexa Browser: a dedicated browser experience where Nexa AI becomes a built-in browser assistant.
 Nexa Labs: the company structure, brand, operating system, and development organization behind the 
products.
 This document: a master startup and development blueprint for new collaborators, future developers, 
sponsors, advisors, and internal planning.

5. What Makes Nexa AI Different
The answer to “Why not just use ChatGPT or Gemini?”
Nexa AI should be explained as a browser-centered assistant ecosystem, not only a general chatbot. Existing AI 
systems are powerful, but users often still need to copy/paste content, manually navigate websites, open 
different tabs, and explain context repeatedly. Nexa AI aims to reduce that friction by connecting the assistant 
more directly to the browsing experience and user task flow.
Difference Meaning for users
Works as chatbot across browsers Users can access Nexa AI from Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Opera, 
and other browsers through the website.
Built-in assistant inside Nexa Browser Inside Nexa Browser, Nexa AI can become more contextual and 
action-oriented.
Page understanding The assistant can help summarize, explain, or analyze the page 
the user is viewing, subject to permissions and privacy controls.
Task execution focus The product is designed around getting things done: writing, 
form filling, summarizing, safe browsing checks, and web 
assistance.
User preference memory With consent, Nexa AI can use saved profile preferences to 
personalize outputs such as resumes, bios, emails, and writing 
style.
Jamaica-born global product The company story is rooted in Jamaica while building for 
global users.
Simple explanation for new people
ChatGPT is mainly a place you go to ask questions. Nexa AI is being built to become a browser pal that helps 
users do tasks across the web and inside Nexa Browser.

7. Nexa Browser Vision
The dedicated browser where Nexa AI becomes deeper and more useful.
Nexa Browser is the planned browser product that integrates Nexa AI directly into the browsing workflow. The 
browser should have a modern interface, a right-side AI assistant panel, a productivity sidebar, workspace tools, 
safe browsing indicators, and permission-based AI access to the current page.
Browser feature Purpose
Nexa AI side panel Persistent assistant that can answer, summarize, write, and 
suggest actions.
Page understanding card Shows what Nexa AI understands about the current page and 
what it can help with.
Smart action buttons One-click actions such as summarize, fill form, write response, 
check safety, export notes.
Workspace sidebar Home, workspace, downloads, history, bookmarks, and AI tools.
Privacy control panel Users control what Nexa AI can see and whether data can be 
stored or used to improve the model.
Safe browsing indicators Helps users understand suspicious domains, insecure pages, 
risky forms, and possible phishing behavior.
Task history Shows completed actions and allows users to review or undo 
when possible.
Design principle
Nexa Browser should not feel like a normal browser with a chatbot pasted beside it. It should feel like the 
browser and the AI were designed together.

13. AI Model and Data Strategy
How Nexa AI should improve without abusing user trust.
The model strategy should begin practical: use available open-weight models or local/API model routes where 
affordable, test small and medium models, and focus on product behavior before expensive training. Fine-tuning 
should happen only after Nexa Labs has clean datasets, user consent systems, evaluation processes, and a clear 
reason for training.
Area Strategy
Model choice Start with small/medium models that can run on available 
hardware; evaluate quality, cost, latency, and safety.
Prompt system Use structured system prompts, task templates, and model 
behavior tests.
RAG/grounding Use retrieval for documents, help center, company knowledge, 
and user-approved files.
User data Do not train on user data unless users clearly opt in.
Feedback data Collect ratings, bug reports, and bad-response examples for 
improvement.
Evaluation Create test sets for resumes, summaries, browser tasks, coding 
help, and safety responses.
Fine-tuning Use only cleaned, permissioned, and documented datasets.
Fallbacks If local model is weak, route certain tasks to better models later 
if budget permits.
AI rule
Nexa AI should become smarter through measured evaluation, clean data, and user trust - not by secretly 
using private conversations.

14. Privacy, Safety, and Trust
The foundation for user confidence.
Because Nexa AI may handle messages, documents, resumes, browsing context, and user preferences, privacy 
must be designed from the beginning. Users should understand what is collected, why it is collected, how long it 
is kept, and how they can delete or export it.
Policy area Nexa Labs direction
Consent Users must choose whether their data can be used to improve 
the model.
Data minimization Collect only what is necessary for the feature.
Deletion Users should be able to delete chats and profile data.
Export Users should be able to export account and chat data later.
Browser context Nexa Browser should ask permission before sending page 
content to AI.
Sensitive data Avoid collecting unnecessary sensitive information.
Safety disclaimers Tell users AI can make mistakes and should not replace 
professional advice.
Admin access Admin/user data access should be limited, logged, and justified.
 Privacy Policy must be published before broad public launch.
 Terms of Use must explain acceptable use and limitations.
 AI Safety Policy must define restricted uses and unsafe tasks.
 Internal Data Handling Policy must guide developers and team members.

15. Security and Browser Risk Controls
Special risk areas for an AI browser assistant.
Browser-integrated AI is powerful but risky. A browser assistant can see pages, suggest form entries, and 
potentially interact with websites. Nexa Labs must build guardrails before allowing advanced actions.
Risk Control
AI sees private page content Require user permission and visible indicators.
Accidental form submission AI can draft/fill, but user must review and manually confirm 
critical actions.
Phishing pages Show warnings for suspicious URLs, risky forms, mismatched 
domains, and insecure pages.
Prompt injection from websites Do not blindly follow instructions from page content. Treat page 
content as untrusted input.
Credential handling Never ask AI to store passwords or secret keys unless a secure 
password manager integration exists.
Payment actions Do not allow autonomous payment or purchase actions in early 
versions.
Admin abuse Use role-based access, logs, and limited permissions.
Extension/browser exploit risk Keep browser integrations sandboxed and regularly updated.
Critical safety principle
Nexa AI can assist with tasks, but users must remain in control of sensitive actions such as payments, 
submissions, account changes, and personal data sharing.

22. Funding, Ownership, and Governance Principles
How Nexa Labs should protect founder control while raising support.
Nexa Labs should avoid giving away too much equity too early. Equity should be based on valuation, 
contribution, commitment, and written agreements. Investors should not automatically receive monthly 
revenue. Shareholders receive dividends only when dividends are declared, and the company should keep 
enough runway before paying dividends.
Principle Recommended rule
Founder control Aim to keep at least 65%-70% control where possible.
First investor round Prefer 5%-15% total equity sold, not 10%-15% to each investor.
Team option pool Reserve 5%-10% for future key team members if needed.
Vesting Any meaningful team equity should vest over time.
Dividends Do not pay dividends until the company has stable runway and 
growth needs covered.
Investor terms Review valuation, voting rights, board seats, liquidation 
preference, anti-dilution, and information rights.
Legal review Do not sign major equity/investor documents without lawyer 
review.
CEO rule
Money alone does not decide ownership. The deal terms decide ownership.
